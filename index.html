Born and raised in Southern California, CD Eskilson is a nonbinary poet, editor, and educator. Their work appears or is forthcoming in the Cortland Review, Peach Mag, Yes Poetry, Moonchild Magazine, Teen Vogue, and After the Pause, among others.

CDâ€™s poetry investigates the legacy of monstrosity ascribed to those at the intersection of mental illness and trans/nonbinary identities. Their work responds to and rewrites popular media narratives to work towards self-acceptance and fight transphobia. In doing so, they hope to plot out neurodivergent, trans, and nonbinary futures.

CD is Poetry Editor of Exposition Review , where they curated the journal's Legacy Flash 405 competition to commemorate the 50th anniversary of the Stonewall Uprising. They were previously an editor at Foothill: a Journal of Poetry. CD is currently a reader for Split Lip Magazine.

They are an alum of Tin House, Winter Tangerine's NYC Workshop, the PEN Emerging Voices Poetry Master Class, and a scholarship recipient from the New York State Summer Writers Institute. They live in Los Angeles and worry about their houseplants.
